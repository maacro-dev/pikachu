description: Guimbal, a preserved colonial town in Iloilo, is 29 km from Iloilo
  City and known for its large, scenic town plaza. Originally called Gibuangan,
  it was later named Guimbal after the small drum (attabal) used by locals to
  warn of Moro raids. The Augustinians established a convento here in 1590,
  adding to its rich cultural heritage. The fourth-class municipality has 33
  barangays, a population of 33,820, celebrates its fiesta on September 10 in
  honor of San Nicolas de Tolentino, and holds market day every Tuesday. It can
  be reached by jeepney from Oton or Iloilo City.
