district: oton
date: 2026-05-01
month: 5
description: >
  Celebrated every last week of April until the first week of May. The
  festival’s name was derived from the older name identifying the ancient
  settlement of Katagman, which eventually became Oton. Celebrated since 2004,
  it showcases Oton’s rich history, from its pre-colonial settlement of Katagman
  to its role as a major trading emporium for indigenous and foreign goods. The
  icon of the celebration is the Chinese Golden Death Mask, worn by performers
  on their foreheads, as a mask, or used as an accessory or hand prop. Seven
  participating tribes are clustered from its 37 barangays.
