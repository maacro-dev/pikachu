The Miagao Church, officially the Santo Tomas de Villanueva Parish Church, is one of four Baroque churches in the Philippines inscribed as UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Built in 1787, its facade depicts a uniquely Filipino interpretation of Christian iconography — coconut palms, papayas, and local flora frame Saint Christopher. Beyond the church, Miagao's weavers produce world-renowned hablon fabric.
